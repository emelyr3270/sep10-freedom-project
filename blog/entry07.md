## Context
In my SEP10 class i have learned many new concepts that ive applied onto my freedom project of animation. After creating my website, we were assigned to create a presentation to show off our effort and knowledge on our project. We had an in-class presentaiton and an expo elevator pitch. In the expo elevator pitch, the sep10 classes had to present their projects in the cafeteria to judges. We were able to work on our slide presentations in class, i wanted to create my slides fun and go a bit beyond. I decided to test out one of the animation softwares that i found when researching my topic an added it onto my website and presentation. 



## Takeaways from my in-class presentation
<p> When i presented in class, i was a bit nervous but was excited to explain my topic of animation. I was able to talk about everything i wanted. What helped me present was practicing infront of people, it helped me think about what i wanted to say and really took away my nervs. </p>
<p>
  One of my Takeaways that i had when presenting in class was to make eyecontact. Although making eye contact may seem hard for others, it really helps grab the audiences attention. It makes them feel that theyŕe actually interacting with your presentation. 
</p>
<p>Another takeaway was to remain calm and be confident. I had a fear of people judging me based on how i presented, but i remembered how hard i worked all year to create my project. I was proud of how far i came, so when presenting i was very excited and confident in showing off what i learned and made. </p>
<p> My last takeaway was forgetting to have a conclusion. I was so worked up in my beginning of my preesentation, that i forgot how to close it off and give the audience a thought about my presentation. </p>

## Takeaways from my expo elevator pitch
<p> In my expo elevator pitch, we were in the cafeteria and i sat on a lunch table with my classmate, Daniil. We were both nervous about presenting, so we decided to be eachothers audience and give feedback. It really helped and gave us a start on how to present and know what to say. By the time the judges came, i was confident and ready to present. I counted a total of 16 people who came up to my table and showed intrest in my presentation.</p>
<p> One of my takeaways were to talk slower. Whenever i present, i tend to talk fast because i get nervous. But this was also because i was excited and had a few minutes to explain my presentation. But one thing that i could do better next time is talk a bit slower so that my audience is able to catch what im saying.
</p>
